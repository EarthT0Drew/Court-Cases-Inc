Details

Overview

      Ellsberg game sensitive documents from the government to the New York Times, but the government obtained a court order to prevent the publishing of them. Therefore, Ellsberg released them to Washington Post, and the government tried to do the same thing, but the court refused. After the government appealed the case, the Supreme court ruled, 6-3, that the prior restraint as unconstitutional.

Backstory

Ellsberg gave these papers, later known as the Pentagon Papers, to the New York Times newspaper company. The government gave a court order preventing NYT from publishing these papers because publishing these papers would "endanger national security" (FindLaw). Ellsberg in turn gave these papers to the Washington Post, also a newspaper company, and the governemnt attempted to get another court order. However, this court order was not passed, and the Government appealed the case to the Supreme Court.

The Decision

After hearing what both sides had to say, the Superme Court rulled, 6-3, that it was unconstitutional for the government to prevent the publication of documents, even if those documents are sensitive.

Aftermath

This case was taken as a victory for the Press, but not everybody agreed with the the Supreme Court desision, beliveing that the government should censor information in order to protect the country. Anyway, now the government is not allowed to prevent the publishing of documents even if they endanger the national security.
